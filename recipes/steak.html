Pan Seared Steak (Wagyu or Tender cuts)

Description

Pan Seared steak is a very simple process and produces the most mouth-watering steaks. Any tender cut of steak will do, well marbled steaks give the best results.

Ingredients

  • Steak
  • Butter
  • Aromatics

Steps

  1. Use paper towels to pat the steaks dry to get a perfect sear and reduce oil splatter.
  2. Just before cooking steaks, sprinkle both sides liberally with salt and pepper.
  3. On medium and brush with oil. Using just 1/2 Tbsp oil reduces splatter.
  4. Add steaks and sear each side 3-4 minutes until a brown crust has formed then use tongs to turn steaks on their sides and sear edges (1 min per edge).
  5. Melt in butter with quartered garlic cloves and rosemary sprigs. Tilt pan to spoon garlic butter over steaks and cook to your desired doneness (see chart below).
  6. Remove steak and rest 10 minutes before slicing against the grain.
